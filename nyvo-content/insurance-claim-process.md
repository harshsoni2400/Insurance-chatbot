# Insurance Claim Process Guide

## Health Insurance Claims

### Types of Health Insurance Claims

#### 1. Cashless Claims
- Treatment at network hospital
- No upfront payment (except non-covered expenses)
- Insurer settles directly with hospital

**Process:**
1. Show health card at hospital
2. Hospital contacts TPA/insurer
3. Pre-authorization received (2-4 hours)
4. Treatment proceeds
5. Final bill settlement at discharge
6. Pay only non-covered items

#### 2. Reimbursement Claims
- Treatment at any hospital (network or non-network)
- Pay first, claim later
- Full or partial reimbursement based on coverage

**Process:**
1. Pay hospital bills
2. Collect all documents
3. Submit claim within time limit (usually 15-30 days)
4. Insurer reviews claim
5. Reimbursement credited to bank account

### Documents Required for Health Insurance Claims

**Standard Documents:**
- Duly filled claim form
- Health card/policy copy
- Photo ID proof
- Hospital discharge summary
- All medical bills and receipts (originals)
- Prescription copies
- Diagnostic test reports
- Doctor's certificate

**For Planned Hospitalization:**
- Pre-authorization request form
- Treating doctor's recommendation

**For Accident Cases:**
- FIR/MLC copy
- Accident report

### Timeline for Health Insurance Claims

| Stage | Typical Duration |
|-------|-----------------|
| Pre-authorization | 2-4 hours |
| Claim submission | Within 15-30 days of discharge |
| Initial review | 7 days |
| Query resolution | 7-15 days |
| Final settlement | 30 days from complete documentation |

### Common Reasons for Health Claim Rejection

1. **Pre-existing disease within waiting period**
2. **Excluded treatment/procedure**
3. **Policy lapsed due to non-payment**
4. **Incorrect or incomplete information**
5. **Treatment at non-network hospital without intimation**
6. **Bills exceeding sub-limits**
7. **Missing documents**
8. **Treatment not medically necessary**

### Tips for Smooth Health Claims

✅ Always carry your health card  
✅ Intimate insurer within 24 hours of hospitalization  
✅ Keep all original bills and prescriptions  
✅ Get itemized bills  
✅ Ensure hospital uses proper codes  
✅ Follow up regularly  
✅ Keep copies of everything submitted

## Term Insurance Claims

### When to File a Term Insurance Claim

A claim is filed when the insured person passes away during the policy term. The nominee (beneficiary) files the claim.

### Steps to File Term Insurance Claim

1. **Immediate Steps**
   - Obtain death certificate
   - Collect policy documents
   - Note policy number and insurer details

2. **Notify Insurer**
   - Call customer care
   - Send written intimation
   - Most insurers have online claim registration

3. **Submit Documents**
   - Fill claim form accurately
   - Attach all required documents
   - Submit within specified time

4. **Investigation (if any)**
   - Insurer may investigate for high-value claims
   - Early claims (within 3 years) may face scrutiny

5. **Claim Settlement**
   - Amount credited to nominee's bank account
   - Or through cheque as per policy

### Documents Required for Term Insurance Claims

**Basic Documents:**
- Original policy document (or indemnity bond if lost)
- Claim form signed by nominee
- Death certificate (original + copies)
- Nominee's ID proof
- Nominee's bank account details
- Cancelled cheque

**Additional Documents Based on Cause of Death:**

*Natural Death:*
- Hospital records (if hospitalized)
- Medical attendant's certificate
- Burial/cremation certificate

*Accidental Death:*
- FIR copy
- Post-mortem report
- Police investigation report
- Newspaper clippings (if applicable)
- Final report/charge sheet

*Death Due to Illness:*
- All medical records
- Treatment history
- Doctor's certificate stating cause of death

### Term Insurance Claim Settlement Timeline

| Stage | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Claim intimation | As soon as possible |
| Document submission | Within 90 days typically |
| Initial review | 7-15 days |
| Investigation (if needed) | 30-90 days |
| Final decision | 30 days from complete documentation |
| Payout | 7 days from approval |

### Reasons for Term Insurance Claim Rejection

1. **Non-disclosure of material facts**
   - Pre-existing health conditions
   - Smoking/alcohol habits
   - Hazardous occupations

2. **Policy lapsed**
   - Premium not paid within grace period

3. **Suicide clause**
   - Suicide within 12 months of policy

4. **Fraud**
   - False statements in application

5. **Excluded causes**
   - Death due to excluded activities

6. **Documentation issues**
   - Missing or inconsistent documents

### What to Do If Claim is Rejected

1. **Get Written Rejection**
   - Ask for detailed reason in writing

2. **Review Policy Terms**
   - Check if rejection is valid

3. **File Grievance**
   - First with insurer's grievance cell
   - Response within 15 days mandatory

4. **Approach IRDAI**
   - Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS)
   - Online complaint portal

5. **Insurance Ombudsman**
   - For claims up to ₹30 lakhs
   - Free and quick resolution

6. **Consumer Court/Civil Court**
   - For larger disputes
   - Legal action as last resort

## Tips for Both Health and Life Insurance Claims

### Before You Need to Claim

1. **Read your policy thoroughly**
2. **Understand what's covered and excluded**
3. **Keep policy documents safe**
4. **Inform family about policies**
5. **Keep nominee details updated**
6. **Save insurer's claim contact numbers**

### During the Claim Process

1. **Document everything**
2. **Keep copies of all submissions**
3. **Note dates and reference numbers**
4. **Follow up in writing (email)**
5. **Don't miss deadlines**
6. **Be honest and accurate**

### Grievance Redressal Options

| Level | Authority | Timeline |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Insurance Company Grievance Cell | 15 days |
| 2 | IRDAI IGMS | 30 days |
| 3 | Insurance Ombudsman | 30-90 days |
| 4 | Consumer Court | Varies |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I file a cashless claim at any hospital?**
A: Only at network hospitals listed by your insurer.

**Q: What if I lose my policy document?**
A: Request duplicate from insurer. Claim can still be processed with verification.

**Q: Is there a time limit to file claims?**
A: Yes, typically 15-30 days for health claims. Term insurance claims should be filed as soon as possible.

**Q: Can a claim be partially paid?**
A: Yes, if some items are not covered or exceed limits, partial payment is made.

**Q: What happens if insurer delays settlement?**
A: IRDAI mandates interest payment for delays beyond 30 days.
