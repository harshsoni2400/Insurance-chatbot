# Health Insurance Basics in India

## What is Health Insurance?

Health insurance is a contract between you and an insurance company where you pay a premium in exchange for coverage of medical expenses. In India, health insurance has become essential due to rising healthcare costs.

## Types of Health Insurance in India

### 1. Individual Health Insurance
- Covers single person
- Premium based on age, health status, and sum insured
- Best for: Young professionals, single individuals

### 2. Family Floater Plans
- Single policy covering entire family (spouse, children, parents)
- Sum insured shared among all members
- Usually more economical than individual plans for each member
- Best for: Families looking for comprehensive coverage

### 3. Senior Citizen Health Insurance
- Designed for people aged 60+
- Higher premium but covers age-related ailments
- Some policies cover up to age 80
- Best for: Elderly parents, retired individuals

### 4. Critical Illness Insurance
- Lump sum payout on diagnosis of specified critical illnesses
- Covers cancer, heart attack, stroke, kidney failure, etc.
- Works alongside regular health insurance
- Best for: Those wanting extra protection against major illnesses

### 5. Group Health Insurance
- Provided by employers to employees
- Lower premiums due to group rates
- Coverage ends when employment ends
- Best for: Corporate employees (as supplement to personal policy)

## Key Terms to Know

### Sum Insured
The maximum amount the insurer will pay during a policy year. Choose based on:
- City of residence (metro cities need higher coverage)
- Family medical history
- Age of insured members
- Lifestyle factors

Recommended minimum:
- Individual in metro city: ₹5-10 lakhs
- Family floater: ₹10-25 lakhs

### Premium
Annual/monthly amount you pay for insurance coverage. Factors affecting premium:
- Age (older = higher premium)
- Sum insured
- Pre-existing conditions
- Zone/city
- Number of members (for floater)

### Waiting Period
Period during which certain conditions are not covered:
- **Initial waiting period**: 30 days from policy start (except accidents)
- **Pre-existing disease waiting period**: 2-4 years typically
- **Specific illness waiting period**: 1-2 years for conditions like hernia, cataract

### Sub-limits
Caps on specific expenses:
- **Room rent**: Daily limit on hospital room charges
- **Disease-wise sub-limits**: Maximum payout for specific diseases
- Tip: Prefer policies with no sub-limits

### Co-payment
Percentage of claim amount you must pay from your pocket.
- Common in senior citizen policies
- Example: 20% co-pay means you pay ₹20,000 on a ₹1 lakh claim

### No Claim Bonus (NCB)
Reward for not making claims:
- Typically 5-50% increase in sum insured per claim-free year
- Cumulative bonus can double your coverage over years
- Some policies offer NCB protection

## What is Covered?

### Typically Covered
- Hospitalization expenses (room, nursing, ICU)
- Pre-hospitalization (30-60 days)
- Post-hospitalization (60-180 days)
- Daycare procedures (procedures not requiring 24-hour stay)
- Ambulance charges
- Domiciliary hospitalization (treatment at home)
- AYUSH treatments (Ayurveda, Yoga, Unani, Siddha, Homeopathy)

### What is NOT Covered (Common Exclusions)
- Cosmetic/plastic surgery
- Dental treatment (unless due to accident)
- Pregnancy and maternity (unless specifically covered)
- Self-inflicted injuries
- War, nuclear events
- Adventure sports injuries
- Drug/alcohol abuse related conditions
- Experimental treatments

## How to Choose Health Insurance

### Step 1: Assess Your Needs
- Family size and ages
- Existing health conditions
- Budget for premiums
- Preferred hospitals

### Step 2: Compare Key Features
- Sum insured options
- Network hospitals in your city
- Claim settlement ratio
- Waiting periods
- Sub-limits and co-pay

### Step 3: Check the Insurer
- Claim settlement ratio (aim for >90%)
- Incurred claim ratio
- Customer reviews
- Network hospital size

### Step 4: Understand the Fine Print
- Exclusions
- Room rent limits
- Pre-existing disease coverage
- Renewal terms

## IRDAI Regulations to Know

The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) has mandated:

1. **Free Look Period**: 15-30 days to review and cancel policy with refund
2. **Portability**: Switch insurers without losing waiting period credit
3. **Lifelong Renewability**: Cannot be denied renewal based on claims
4. **Standardized Exclusions**: Common exclusion list across all insurers
5. **Cashless Treatment**: At network hospitals
6. **Grievance Redressal**: Time-bound claim settlement (30 days)

## Tips for Smart Health Insurance Buying

1. **Buy early**: Lower premiums when young, no pre-existing conditions
2. **Don't underinsure**: Medical inflation is 10-15% annually
3. **Read exclusions carefully**: Know what's not covered
4. **Check renewal terms**: Ensure lifelong renewability
5. **Compare claim settlement ratios**: Higher is better
6. **Network hospital coverage**: Ensure your preferred hospitals are covered
7. **Consider top-up plans**: Affordable way to increase coverage
8. **Don't hide information**: Full disclosure prevents claim rejection
